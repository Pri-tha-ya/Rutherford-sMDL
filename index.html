Rutherford's Model of Atom

Introduction

In 1911, Ernest Rutherford proposed a revolutionary atomic model after his famous gold foil experiment. This model replaced the plum pudding idea and introduced the concept of a nucleus.

Key Features

According to Rutherford:

  • The atom has a small, dense, positively charged nucleus.
  • Electrons revolve around the nucleus in orbits.
  • Most of the atom is empty space.
